Centre ville-orléans

Orléans is a French commune located on the banks of the Loire, prefecture of the Loiret department and capital of the Centre-Val de Loire region. Located in the heart of the Loire Valley and at the gateway to the natural regions of the Orléans forest, Sologne and Beauce, and approximately 120 kilometers south of Paris, In 2021, Orléans is the most populous municipality in the Centre-Val de Loire region after Tours Tours with 116,617 inhabitants.

The history of Orléans presents the history of the French city of Orléans. The city's rich historical past is due in particular to the fact that it constitutes a strategic crossing point on the Loire because it is located at its northernmost point, therefore closest to Paris.

Region: Center Val De Loire

The different important places to visit in my neighborhood and the most photographed :

The Holy Cross Cathedral

Located on the square that bears its name, the Sainte-Croix Cathedral is a remarkable monument in many ways. Don't miss out on visiting it during your stay in Orléans. Indeed, this building has a special history. It is a curiosity to be observed closely. It was Louis XIV who ordered it and, to thank him, A sun in the colors of sunrise and sunset was added to the stained glass windows. A visit to this emblematic place will not fail to delight you.

The Groslot Hotel

Located on Place de L'Etape, the Groslot hotel is a private mansion built in the 16th century at the request of Jacques Groslot, bailiff of Orléans. This magnificent building of Renaissance architecture has been classified as a historic monument since 1862. Becoming the town hall after the French Revolution, it is today a private mansion dedicated to the celebration of weddings. In order to fully understand the history of this emblematic place, there is the Geomotif application which reveals its secrets to you through the different motifs contained in the building.

Place du Matroi: statue of Jeanne d'Arc

The main square in downtown Orléans, Place du Martroi is a must if you visit the capital of the Centre-Val-de-Loire region. This square is truly part of the history of the city into which it was integrated during the construction of the second enclosure in the 14th century. Place du Martroi is today the most important place in the city, with in its center the famous equestrian statue of Joan of Arc created by the sculptor Foyatier, and representing “the virgin” giving thanks to God. On the square, don't miss the Maison de la Chancellerie, a building classified as a historic monument..

Beaux-arts museum

Located on rue Fernand Rabier, the Musée des Beaux-Arts d’Orléans will not fail to delight you. It is one of the richest and oldest museums in France. Its permanent collection presents works of art ranging from the 15th century to the 20th century. The Orléans Museum of Fine Arts contains a total of 2,000 paintings and 700 sculptures, but also a vast collection including around 10,000 drawings and 50,000 prints. These exhibitions are sometimes linked to other museums, which makes it possible to broaden artistic discoveries. A visit to the Orléans Museum of Fine Arts is as fascinating as it is enriching.

The House of Jeanne d'Arc

A real must-see, the Joan of Arc house is the place where the famous heroine of French history stayed during the siege of Orléans, while she contributed to the liberation of the city. It is a beautiful town house of medieval architecture with its half-timbered facade, red bricks and mullioned and stained-glass windows. The Jeanne d'Arc museum thus houses a documentary collection dedicated to the heroine and her era including more than 37,000 documents.
